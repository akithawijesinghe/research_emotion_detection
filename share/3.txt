⭐ FEATURE EXTRACTION USING TF-IDF (full explanation)

After cleaning the dataset, the emotional text comments were converted into numerical form using the TF-IDF (Term Frequency–Inverse Document Frequency) vectorizer.
TF-IDF assigns higher importance to words that are frequent within a comment but rare across the whole dataset.

This step transforms raw text into a machine-understandable format.

Parameters used

You applied the following settings:

Parameter	Value	Meaning
ngram_range	(1,2)	Uses unigrams and bigrams (1-word and 2-word phrases)
max_features	5000	Keep only top 5000 most important features
stop_words	"english"	Removes common English words like "the", "is", "and"
split	80% train / 20% test	Stratified to keep emotion distribution equal
Output

TF-IDF produced two sparse matrices:

X_train_tfidf shape → (4876, 5000)

X_test_tfidf shape → (1220, 5000)

Interpretation:

4876 training samples

1220 testing samples

each sample represented by 5000 features

These matrices are used by all machine-learning models (Logistic Regression, SVM, Random Forest, etc.)

You also saved the TF-IDF vectorizer to ensure consistent transformation during model deployment.
