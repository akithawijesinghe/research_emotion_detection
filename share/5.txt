📄 Paragraph for “Results & Discussion”

To understand the initial performance of traditional machine-learning algorithms on the emotion detection task, four baseline classifiers were trained: Logistic Regression, Multinomial Naive Bayes, Linear Support Vector Classifier, and Random Forest.

Figure 4.1 presents the test-set accuracy of each model. Logistic Regression achieved the highest accuracy (0.5229), followed closely by Random Forest (0.5000). Linear SVC and Multinomial Naive Bayes performed slightly lower on this dataset. Logistic Regression was selected as the baseline model for further tuning due to its simplicity, stability, and strong performance with TF-IDF text features.

Figure 4.2 shows the confusion matrix for the Logistic Regression model. The diagonal values indicate the correctly classified instances, while off-diagonal values represent misclassifications. The model performed well on sadness and anger but struggled with fear and anxiety, which were often confused due to linguistic similarity in short YouTube comments. This analysis provides the foundation for improvements through hyperparameter tuning and error analysis.
