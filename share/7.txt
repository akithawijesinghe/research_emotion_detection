📄 Suggested Error Analysis Paragraph (paste into thesis)

To better understand the limitations of the tuned Logistic Regression model, we extracted 100 randomly selected misclassified instances from the test set (Appendix X). Each record includes the original YouTube comment, the true emotion label, and the predicted label. These samples reveal several recurring patterns in model failures.

Many misclassifications occurred in short or ambiguous comments where emotional tone is difficult to detect (e.g., single-word responses or vague reactions). Another common source of errors involved overlap between semantically related emotions, particularly fear, anxiety, and anger. These emotions often co-occur in online discussions, leading the model to confuse categories with similar linguistic cues.

Some failures were due to missing context, sarcasm, or multi-emotion expressions. Manual review showed that a portion of errors may also be attributed to annotation noise or ambiguous labeling. The findings from this analysis highlight opportunities for future work such as using contextual embeddings (e.g., BERT) or multi-label emotion classification.

After evaluating the tuned model, we extracted misclassified test-set predictions by comparing the predicted emotion label against the true annotated label. All incorrectly classified samples were collected, and 100 were selected for manual qualitative analysis. This process was performed programmatically by filtering prediction results from the tuned model and writing them to the file misclassified_sample_100_tuned.csv. These samples were used to categorize common error types and understand the model’s failure patterns.
